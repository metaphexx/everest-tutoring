# Product

## Register

split: brand for `/` and marketing surfaces, product for `/book`, `/dashboard`, `/admin`, `/tutor`

## Users

**Primary decision-maker: HSHS parents** (Year 8–10 students, southwest Perth).

Two arrival modes:

- **Warm arrivals** (school comms, word of mouth): already sold on the program. They want to enrol fast and trust the process is smooth. Friction is the enemy.
- **Research arrivals** (Google, referral): comparison-shopping tutoring options. They'll weigh price, schedule, credibility, and brand feel. They need to be convinced before they book.

Students are the subject of the service but not the primary user; parents hold the card and make the decision. Design for the parent first.

## Product Purpose

An enrolment and class management platform for the **Everest Tutoring × Harrisdale Senior High School** afterschool program. Parents book their Year 8–10 children into weekly English, Maths, and Science sessions held on-campus at HSHS. Pricing is per-subject with prorated term fees and a sibling discount.

Success means: a parent lands, understands the offer in under 30 seconds, books without confusion, and feels good recommending it at school pickup.

This is also **Everest Tutoring's first flagship digital product** — it will serve as the brand foundation the main website is rebuilt from. Design choices here should scale.

## Brand Personality

Professional yet approachable. Modern and elegant. No fluff, no AI slop.

Three-word summary: **confident, clear, human**

Emotional goals (all four, in tension — hold them together):
- **Relief**: "I found something I trust for my child" — every screen removes doubt
- **Pride**: "My child is going to a premium program" — elevated without being cold
- **Confidence**: "My child is going to do better" — competence communicated, not claimed
- **Excitement**: "This is exactly what we needed" — fresh energy, not generic tutoring-centre vibes

Voice: direct and warm. Speaks like a knowledgeable colleague, not a salesperson. Never corporate, never patronising.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing pages**: hero metric cards, gradient text on headings, glassmorphism decorations, identical icon-grid feature sections. The AI-made aesthetic.
- **Cheap tutoring centres**: cluttered layouts, stock photo overload, dated typography, trust-badge spam ("5000+ students!").
- **Corporate/cold**: authoritative but sterile. Big consulting firm energy. Zero warmth or personality.
- **Over-branded school material**: HSHS logo repeated everywhere, clipart, very formal school-newsletter tone. This is a premium service, not a school notice.
- **The old Everest website**: explicitly not the reference. Start fresh.

## Design Principles

1. **Premium without pretension.** Elevated aesthetics that feel attainable, not exclusive. Parents should feel the quality without feeling judged.
2. **Trust through transparency.** Pricing is shown upfront, steps are clear, nothing is buried. Relief comes from knowing exactly what you're getting before you commit.
3. **Quiet confidence.** No shouting. No gradient headlines. No hero metrics. Let the product quality speak — the design holds space for the content, it doesn't compete with it.
4. **School-contextual, not school-branded.** The HSHS partnership is real and should feel grounded, but this is a premium service that happens to be at the school. Don't let it look like a school website.
5. **Foundation, not just a funnel.** This is where the Everest brand starts. Decisions here will ripple to the main site — prefer choices that can grow rather than ones that solve only the immediate problem.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Good contrast ratios on all text, keyboard-navigable booking flow, focus-visible states throughout. Sensible font sizes (body ≥ 16px). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations added.
